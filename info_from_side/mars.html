Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often referred to as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance, which is a result of iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It is about half the size of Earth and has a thin atmosphere, composed mainly of carbon dioxide. Mars features the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, which is nearly three times the height of Mount Everest. Additionally, it has a large canyon system known as Valles Marineris, which is over 2,500 miles (4,000 kilometers) long.
Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, which are thought to be captured asteroids. The planet has polar ice caps made of water and dry ice (frozen carbon dioxide) that change with the seasons. Mars has been a major target for exploration, with various missions sending rovers and landers to study its geology, climate, and potential for past life.

Some of the missions on Mars are :

Perseverance is a car-sized rover that landed on Mars in February 2021 as part of NASA's Mars 2020 mission. Its main goal is to search for signs of ancient life and collect rock and soil samples for potential return to Earth. Perseverance also carries the Ingenuity helicopter, which has performed the first powered flights on another planet, testing new technologies for future aerial exploration.

Curiosity is a car-sized rover that has been exploring Mars since its landing in August 2012. It is equipped with a variety of scientific instruments to analyze the planet's surface and atmosphere. Curiosity's mission is to assess whether Mars ever had the conditions to support microbial life and to study the planet's climate and geology.

Mangalyaan, India's first interplanetary mission, was launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in November 2013 and entered Mars' orbit in September 2014. The mission aims to explore the Martian surface and atmosphere, with a focus on studying methane levels and the planet's geology.

Phobos

Phobos is the larger and closer of Mars' two moons and is one of the smallest moons in the solar system. It orbits Mars at an extremely close distance, completing a full orbit in just 7 hours and 39 minutes, making it one of the few moons that rises in the west and sets in the east. Phobos is heavily cratered and has a very irregular shape, resembling a lumpy potato.
Its surface is covered in dust and loose rocks, and it has a large groove system, which is thought to be caused by tidal forces from Mars. Phobos is slowly spiraling inward and is predicted to either crash into Mars or break apart, forming a ring system in the next 50 million years.

Some of the missions on Phobos are :

Phobos-Grunt was a Russian mission aimed at returning samples from Phobos, but it failed to leave Earth orbit. Despite its failure, the mission aimed to study Phobos and Mars in more detail.

Mars Sample Return is a proposed mission to collect samples from Mars and return them to Earth, including potential samples from Phobos. The mission aims to bring back evidence of past life and study Martian geology directly.
