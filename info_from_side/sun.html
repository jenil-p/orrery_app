Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system, and it is the most important source of energy for life on Earth. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, heated to temperatures of millions of degrees at its core.
The Sun accounts for about 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system and provides the gravitational pull that keeps the planets in orbit.
Its energy output, which is essential for life, powers weather patterns and ocean currents on Earth.

Some of the missions to study the Sun are:

SOHO is a project of international cooperation between ESA and NASA, launched in 1995. It has provided a continuous view of the Sun, studying its internal structure, atmosphere, and solar wind. SOHO has greatly enhanced our understanding of solar activity and its impact on the solar system.

Aditya-L1 is India's first mission to study the Sun, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 2023. The spacecraft is designed to observe the Sun's outermost layers (the photosphere and chromosphere) and the solar corona. Aditya-L1 will help scientists better understand solar activities and their impact on space weather, including solar flares and coronal mass ejections that can affect satellites and power grids on Earth.

Parker Solar Probe is a NASA spacecraft launched in 2018 to study the outer corona of the Sun. It is the first spacecraft to fly into the Sun's atmosphere, providing unprecedented data on solar wind, magnetic fields, and solar energetic particles.

SDO was launched in 2010 and observes the Sun in multiple wavelengths. It provides detailed images and data about solar activity, helping scientists understand the Sun's influence on Earth and space weather.

ACE is a NASA spacecraft launched in 1997 that studies particles of solar, interstellar, interplanetary, and galactic origins. It provides critical data on solar particle events and helps in understanding the solar cycle.
