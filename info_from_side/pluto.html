Pluto

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet of the solar system, is now classified as a dwarf planet. It has a diameter of about 1,430 miles (2,302 kilometers) and is primarily composed of ice and rock. Pluto has a thin atmosphere that expands as it gets closer to the Sun. It has five known moons, with Charon being the largest, sharing a unique relationship with Pluto.

Some of the missions on Pluto are :

New Horizons was the first spacecraft to fly by Pluto, providing stunning images and data about its surface, atmosphere, and moons.

Charon

Charon is Pluto's largest moon, and it is almost half the size of Pluto itself, leading to a unique binary system. The two bodies are tidally locked, meaning the same side of Charon always faces Pluto. Charon has a dark polar cap and is believed to have a young surface, indicating possible geological activity.

Some of the missions on Charon are :

New Horizons provided detailed images and data about Charon's surface and its geological features.
