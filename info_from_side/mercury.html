Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the solar system. It is a terrestrial planet with a rocky surface and has no atmosphere to speak of, resulting in extreme temperature fluctuations between day and night. Mercury's surface is heavily cratered, resembling the Moon.
It has a very weak magnetic field and is composed mainly of iron. One of Mercury's most notable features is its large core, which accounts for a significant portion of its mass. Despite being close to the Sun, Mercury has been visited by only one spacecraft, Mariner 10, in the 1970s.

Some of the missions on Mercury are :

Mariner 10 was the first spacecraft to visit Mercury, conducting three flybys and providing the first close-up images of the planet's surface. The mission helped scientists learn about Mercury's geology and atmosphere.

MESSENGER (MErcury Surface, Space Environment, Geochemistry, and Ranging) was the first spacecraft to orbit Mercury. It provided detailed images and data on the planet's surface, magnetic field, and geological history.
